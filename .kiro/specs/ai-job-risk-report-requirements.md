# Requirements Document: 2028 Agentic AI Workforce Disruption Report

## 1. Project Background
The current narrative surrounding Artificial Intelligence is dominated by the "Copilot" myth—the assumption that AI will merely assist human workers in their existing roles. This narrative is fundamentally flawed. As we approach the "Agentic Shift" (2026–2028), we are witnessing the rise of multi-agent networks capable of autonomous execution, self-correction, and state management. 

Existing research is largely speculative, academic, or overly optimistic. There is a critical gap for a forensic, data-driven audit that maps exactly which tasks AI will take and which roles will be eliminated. 

We are building this report to:
1. **Establish Authority:** Position hasanjaffal.com as the definitive source for AI disruption data.
2. **Drive Engagement:** Create a highly citable "link-bait" asset that journalists and HR influencers cannot ignore.
3. **Provide Clarity:** Give professionals a clear, unvarnished look at their future viability in the workforce.

## 2. Objective
Create a centralized, comprehensive master report that evaluates 154 specific job roles and their vulnerability to agentic AI by the year 2028. The final output must serve as a high-value asset that drives organic search traffic, media citations, and professional discussion.

## 3. Content Requirements

### 3.1 Tone & Perspective
* **Direct & Authoritative:** Remove corporate "fluff" and optimistic jargon.
* **Aggressive & Unvarnished:** State the reality of role elimination clearly.
* **Human-Centric:** Write for the reader, not for a board of directors. Use direct, accessible language.
* **Scientific & Analytical:** Focus on data, timelines, and logic.

### 3.2 Structure & Chapter Descriptions
The report will follow a linear, cohesive flow designed for readability as a comprehensive master document.

#### Chapter I: Executive Directive
* **Purpose:** Sets the stage and the core thesis.
* **Includes:** Definition of "Agentic Shift," the "Big Freeze" (hiring trends), the binary outcome (Strategy vs. Execution), and the urgency of the 2028 timeline.

#### Chapter II: State of the 2028 Workforce (Macro-Analysis)
* **Purpose:** Provides scientific macro-context.
* **Includes:** The "Disruption Cliff" analysis (2026-2027), sector-wide impact heatmaps, role compression theory (stripping the execution layer), and alignment with external macro-data.

#### Chapter III: The Threat Matrix (The 154-Role Repository)
* **Purpose:** Acts as the primary evidence repository.
* **Includes:** A unified master table for quick reference, followed by structured modules for each of the 154 roles (Elimination Index, "What AI Takes," "Human Moats," and 24-month countdowns).

#### Chapter IV: Strategic Adaptation (The Pivot)
* **Purpose:** Provides actionable value to the reader.
* **Includes:** Roadmap for transitioning from execution to strategy, a human skills inventory (un-automatable skills), and a 30-day self-audit checklist.

#### Chapter V: Methodology
* **Purpose:** Establishes credibility for journalists.
* **Includes:** Forensic audit criteria, definitions of "Execution" vs. "Judgment" layers, and transparency regarding data inputs and predictive model limitations.

### 3.3 Visual & Data Requirements
* **Standardized Layouts:** Consistent visual modules for every job role (Elimination Index, Task vs. Role gauges, 24-month countdowns).
* **Embeddable Graphics:** High-contrast charts (Histograms of risk, Task-Replacement Heatmaps, Role Compression radar charts) that are ready for journalistic reproduction.

## 4. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
* **SEO Rankings:** Achieve #1 rankings for "Will AI replace [Job Title]" search queries.
* **Backlink Velocity:** High volume of citations from tech journalists and industry newsletters.
* **Content Engagement:** High time-on-page and bounce rate reduction across job-specific audit pages.

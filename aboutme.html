ABOUT

Hasan Jaffal

Data and business intelligence leader. I write about where AI, dashboards, data signals, and leadership fail in real operations — especially under risk.

What I do here

This site is built around four positions — beliefs I hold about how AI, data, and decision-making actually work in operations. Each position is backed by articles, field experience, and uncomfortable trade-offs.

POSITION 01 AI exposes weak operations POSITION 02 Signals need authority POSITION 03 Reporting is not intelligence POSITION 04 AI is changing the skill floor

The problem I focus on

Most analytics work fails for a simple reason: it stops at reporting. A dashboard can show what happened. It does not guarantee action. It does not assign ownership. It does not decide which signal matters, which risk should be escalated, or which operational behavior needs to change.

I write about that gap — between intelligence and action — and what strong teams do to close it.

Background

I lead data and business intelligence in operational environments. My work sits at the intersection of AI, risk detection, fraud analytics, and loss prevention — in contexts where decisions have real consequences and speed matters more than precision.

I am not an academic. I am not a consultant. I am an operator who writes about what works and what fails when data meets real pressure.
